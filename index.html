What is Nexus Radar?

It is an automated Open-Source Intelligence (OSINT) pipeline designed to bridge the gap between local environmental observations and global policy intervention.

Autonomous Scanning

The engine runs daily, scraping the web for specific keywords indicating environmental degradation, animal behavioral anomalies, and localized societal shifts.

5-Layer AI Pipeline

Powered by Google Gemini, the AI validates the signal, extracts affected elements, assesses catastrophe risk, and analyzes the root cause (anthropogenic vs natural).

Actionable Synthesis

Raw data is compiled into a high-level Intelligence Resume every quarter, providing policymakers with direct geographic hotspots and intervention targets.

Why do "Weak Signals" matter?

Traditional disaster management is reactive. It mobilizes resources only after a crisis—like a category 5 hurricane or massive forest fire—has already destroyed infrastructure and lives.

But crises rarely happen overnight. They are preceded by subtle, creeping anomalies known as Latent Signals. A drop in well water pressure today is the drought famine of next year. An isolated 'test burn' in a forest is the regional haze of next month. Nexus Radar catches the breadcrumbs before the explosion.

  • Shifts the paradigm from reactive rescue to proactive intervention.
  • Saves significant economic capital by preventing systemic collapse.
  • Empowers policymakers with data to stop anthropogenic hazards early.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the engine and data.

Where does the data come from?
The data is aggregated autonomously by an AI script utilizing Google Search Grounding. It scours local news, NGO reports, academic pre-prints, and community forums based on a rotating list of highly specific "anomaly keywords." We do not generate fake news; all signals come with verified original source URLs.
What is the 'Threat Score'?
The Threat Score (Priority Score) is an algorithmic calculation from 0-100. It combines the Catastrophe Risk level (40%), the Threat Impact scale (40%), and the Escalation Urgency (20%). A score above 80 triggers a Critical Flag, meaning the system expects severe irreversible damage if ignored.
How often is the Intelligence Resume updated?
While the Signal Inventory (raw data) is updated daily, the Intelligence Resume is a synthesis report generated automatically every 90 days (Quarterly). The AI reads the entire database to find overarching geographic threats, root cause trends, and formulates strategic intervention recommendations.
Can I export the data for my own research?
Yes! Inside the radar dashboard, there is an "Export OSINT Data" button at the top right. It allows you to download the entire dataset formatted in standard DCMI (Dublin Core Metadata Initiative) XML, ready to be imported into QGIS, academic databases, or other analytical tools.
